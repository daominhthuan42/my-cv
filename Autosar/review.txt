# 5. Functional Safety (ISO 26262)

1. What is ISO 26262?
2. What are ASIL levels?
3. What is a safety mechanism?
A safety mechanism is a feature or technique used to detect, prevent, or control faults in order to maintain system safety.

4. What is the difference between systematic faults and random faults?
Systematic faults are caused by design or development errors such as software bugs, incorrect requirements, or integration mistakes. They are usually reproducible under the same conditions.
Random faults are caused by unpredictable hardware failures such as bit flips, electrical noise, or component aging, and are typically handled using safety mechanisms like ECC, watchdogs, and diagnostics

6. What Functional Safety activities have you worked on?
7. What is a safety work product?
10. What is traceability in Functional Safety?
11. How do you verify safety requirements?
Safety requirements are verified by ensuring that:
the implemented system behavior
matches the defined safety requirements
under normal and fault conditions.

In automotive projects following ISO 26262, verification is performed through:
reviews
analysis
testing
traceability
safety validation activities

12. What is the V-model in automotive development?
13. What is the difference between unit testing and integration testing?
14. How do you handle safety-critical bugs?
Safety-critical bugs must be handled with high priority because they may impact vehicle safety in automotive systems following ISO 26262.

15. What risks can occur if SPI communication times out?
If SPI communication times out, risks may include data loss, incorrect system behavior, task blocking.
In automotive systems, SPI timeout handling is important because failed communication with sensors or external devices may lead to unsafe system behavior.

## SPI

1. Explain how SPI communication works.
2. What are MOSI, MISO, SCLK, and CS?
3. What causes SPI communication failure?
4. How do you handle SPI timeout errors?
5. What is full-duplex communication?
6. What is the difference between polling and interrupt mode in SPI?
7. What is DMA support in SPI?
DMA support in SPI allows data transfer between memory and the SPI peripheral without continuous CPU involvement.
The CPU configures the DMA controller once, and DMA automatically handles data movement, improving performance and reducing CPU load, especially for large or high-speed SPI transfers.

2. How do timers work in microcontrollers?
Timers in microcontrollers work by using a hardware counter that increments or decrements based on a clock source.
They are used to:
measure time

create periodic interrupts

Timers are one of the most important peripherals in embedded systems.
